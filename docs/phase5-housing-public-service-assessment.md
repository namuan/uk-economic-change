# Phase 5 assessment: housing and public-service expansion

This note records the Phase 5 source review and implementation decision.

## Summary

Phase 5 adds one broader public-service indicator and documents the remaining housing and public-service coverage gaps.

We added an A&E performance indicator for England. It uses NHS England monthly A&E Attendances and Emergency Admissions data. The indicator measures the percentage of A&E attendances discharged, admitted or transferred within 4 hours.

We did not add a UK-wide housing affordability indicator. The current ONS house price to earnings ratio is consistent for England and Wales. Scotland and Northern Ireland have official house price data, but not a directly comparable official price-to-earnings ratio using the same method and coverage.

We reviewed social care, local authority spending power, courts and schools. We did not add these as Phase 5 indicators because none gave a sufficiently clean 2007-to-latest comparison for this evidence pack. The main barriers were method breaks, changes in responsibilities, or a current series that starts after 2007.

## Housing affordability coverage

The current report uses the ONS ratio of median house price to median workplace-based earnings. This covers England and Wales.

Scotland has official house price data from Registers of Scotland. The series includes median and mean residential sales prices and goes back to April 2003. It does not provide a directly comparable house price to earnings ratio.

Scotland also has Scottish Government housing statistics. These cover new housebuilding, affordable housing supply, rents, stock and tenure. They are useful for housing supply analysis, but they do not fill the same affordability measure.

Northern Ireland has official house price data through the Northern Ireland House Price Index and related official statistics. It does not provide a directly comparable ONS-style median house price to workplace earnings ratio in the same series.

## Housing implementation decision

We have not constructed a UK-wide or four-nation housing affordability ratio in this phase.

The reason is comparability. Combining separate house price sources with separate earnings sources would create a derived measure with method differences across nations. That would be weaker than the current England and Wales ONS measure.

The housing gap remains unresolved and documented. A future extension could add one of these instead:

- a four-nation housing supply indicator, such as annual completions
- separate country-level affordability indicators with separate caveats
- local authority affordability for England and Wales only

## Public-service indicator added

We added A&E four-hour performance for England.

Source: NHS England A&E Attendances and Emergency Admissions monthly time series.

Measure: percentage of all A&E attendances discharged, admitted or transferred within 4 hours.

Baseline: 2011 annual average. This is the nearest defensible full-year baseline because the current monthly time series starts in November 2010.

Latest: 2025 annual average. This avoids comparing a full-year baseline with a partial 2026 year.

Result: performance fell from 96.8% in 2011 to 74.7% in 2025, a fall of 22.1 percentage points.

## Caveats

The A&E indicator is England only.

Monthly values before June 2015 are estimates from weekly data. NHS England says these estimates were produced to support monthly comparison.

Between May 2019 and May 2023, four-hour performance data excludes 14 trusts that took part in the clinical review of access standards field test. NHS England says users should take care when comparing this period with other months.

The indicator measures emergency care access pressure. It is not a whole-public-service pressure measure.

## Public-service candidates reviewed but not added

We reviewed 4 further public-service areas after adding A&E performance.

| Area | Main official source reviewed | Decision | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Adult social care | NHS Digital and DHSC adult social care activity and finance reports | Do not add in Phase 5 | The official series is relevant and annual, but the collections changed over time. Older returns include RAP, PSS-EX1 and related collections. Later releases use SALT, ASC-FR and, from 2024 to 2025, Client Level Data. A 2007-to-latest comparison would mix collection systems and risk treating method change as service change. |
| Local authority spending power | MHCLG local authority revenue expenditure and financing statistics | Do not add in Phase 5 | Revenue spending statistics are official and long-running, but they are not a direct service-pressure measure. Comparisons across 2007 to latest are affected by changes in local authority responsibilities, grants, retained business rates, council tax, fees and accounting treatment. A cash spending series would need extra price and needs adjustment before it could support a clear claim. |
| Courts | Ministry of Justice criminal court statistics | Do not add in Phase 5 | The current criminal court statistics quarterly collection starts in 2014. Earlier court statistics exist, but a 2007-to-latest backlog or timeliness comparison would need reconciliation across predecessor publications and definitions. The series is not yet ready for an automated, reproducible indicator in this pack. |
| Schools | Department for Education school workforce statistics | Do not add in Phase 5 | Pupil to teacher ratios are relevant, but the current school workforce census series starts in 2010 to 2011. That misses the 2007 baseline. School workforce measures also need to be interpreted alongside pupil numbers and school phase, so a single headline pressure claim would be weaker than the current NHS indicators. |

## Public-service implementation decision

We keep A&E four-hour performance as the only new Phase 5 public-service indicator.

We do not add social care, local authority spending power, courts or schools in Phase 5. Each area remains a candidate for a future, separate extension if it starts with a source-specific methodology note.

Future work could add one of these measures if the scope is narrowed:

- adult social care activity, using a post-2014 baseline only
- adult social care finance, with a clear price adjustment and method-break note
- Crown Court outstanding cases, using a post-2014 baseline only
- school pupil to teacher ratios, using a 2010 to 2011 baseline only
- school class sizes, if the source review confirms a consistent 2007-to-latest series
